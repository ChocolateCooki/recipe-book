Sri Lankan Beef Curry

A rich, slow-cooked curry with beef, spices, and coconut milk.

Ingredient List:

    For the Curry:

  • Beef: 500 g, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Onion: 1 large, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, grated
  • Curry leaves: 8-10
  • Green chilies: 2, sliced
  • Tomato: 1 large, chopped
  • Coconut milk: 1 cup (thick)
  • Water or beef stock: 1/2 cup
  • Oil: 2-3 tbsp (preferably coconut oil)

    • For the Spice Mix:

  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Chili powder: 1-2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Roasted curry powder: 1 tbsp (Sri Lankan curry powder preferred)
  • Coriander powder: 1 tsp
  • Black pepper powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Salt: to taste

    • For Tempering:

  • Mustard seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1/4 tsp
  • Cardamom pods: 2-3
  • Cloves: 2-3
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece

Method:

  1. Wash and drain the beef pieces. Marinate with turmeric powder, chili powder, and a pinch of salt. Set aside for 20-30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot or heavy-bottomed pan, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add fenugreek seeds, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and curry leaves. Fry until aromatic.
  3. Add the chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Saut√© until the onions are golden and softened. Stir in the green chilies.
  4. Add the marinated beef to the pot and mix well. Add the roasted curry powder, coriander powder, and black pepper. Stir to coat the beef evenly.
  5. Add the chopped tomato and mix well. Pour in water or beef stock, cover, and simmer over low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Once the beef is tender and the liquid has reduced slightly, pour in the thick coconut milk. Simmer uncovered for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the curry to thicken.
  7. Taste the curry and adjust salt and spice levels as needed.
  8. Serve the Sri Lankan Beef Curry hot with steamed rice, roti, or hoppers, accompanied by sambols or pickles.
