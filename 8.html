Malu Paan

A combination of fish curry stuffed inside bread, often enjoyed with a side of spicy sambol.

Ingredient List:

  • All-purpose flour: 4 cups
  • Instant yeast: 2 tsp
  • Sugar: 2 tbsp
  • Salt: 1 tsp
  • Warm milk: 1/2 cup
  • Warm water: 1/2 cup
  • Egg: 1 large (or 2 tbsp of oil for an egg-free version)
  • Butter: 2 tbsp (softened)

    • For the Filling:

  • Canned tuna or cooked fish: 200 g (drained and flaked)
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Green chilies: 1-2, finely chopped
  • Curry leaves: 8-10, finely chopped
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Chili powder: 1 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Pepper: 1/2 tsp
  • Salt: to taste
  • Lemon juice: 1 tsp
  • Oil: 2 tbsp

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Add the warm milk, warm water, egg, and softened butter. Mix to form a soft dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions, garlic, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until softened and fragrant. Add the turmeric powder, chili powder, pepper, and salt. Stir well. Mix in the flaked fish and cook for 2-3 minutes. Add lemon juice and adjust seasoning as needed. Allow the filling to cool completely before using
  3. Punch down the dough and divide it into equal portions (about 10-12 balls). Roll out each portion into a circle (about 4-5 inches in diameter). Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of the dough circle. Fold the edges over the filling and seal tightly, shaping into a triangle or oval bun. Make sure there are no gaps for the filling to leak out.
  4. Place the shaped buns on a baking tray lined with parchment paper, seam side down. Cover with a cloth and let them rise again for 30-45 minutes.
  5. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Brush the buns with egg wash or milk for a golden finish. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the buns are golden brown.
  6. Allow the buns to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them warm or at room temperature with a cup of tea.
