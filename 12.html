Dhal Curry

A comforting and flavorful lentil curry made with yellow lentils, cooked with spices and coconut milk, commonly served with rice.

Ingredient List:

    For the Curry:

  • Split red lentils (masoor dhal): 1 cup
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Salt: to taste
  • Water: 2 cups
  • Coconut milk: 1 cup (thick)

    • For Tempering:

  • Oil: 2-3 tbsp (preferably coconut oil)
  • Mustard seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1/4 tsp
  • Curry leaves: 8-10
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely sliced
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Green chilies: 2-3, sliced lengthwise
  • Tomato: 1 small, chopped
  • Dried red chilies: 2 (optional)
  • Chili powder: 1/2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Cumin powder: 1/2 tsp

Method:

  1. Wash the lentils thoroughly until the water runs clear. In a medium pot, combine lentils, turmeric powder, and water. Cook over medium heat until the lentils are soft and fully cooked (about 15-20 minutes). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Add salt to taste and mix in the coconut milk. Let it simmer for another 5 minutes. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and allow them to splutter. Add fenugreek seeds, curry leaves, and dried red chilies (if using). Saut√© briefly until aromatic. Add the sliced onions and garlic. Cook until the onions are golden and soft. Stir in green chilies, tomatoes, chili powder, and cumin powder. Cook until the tomatoes are softened and the mixture is fragrant.
  3. Pour the tempering mixture over the cooked lentils. Stir well to combine. Simmer the curry for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Taste the curry and adjust salt or spices if needed.
  5. Serve the Pol Dhal Curry warm with steamed rice, roti, or bread.
