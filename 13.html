Kottu Roti

Kottu Roti is a popular Sri Lankan street food made by stir-frying chopped roti with vegetables, meat, and spices.

Ingredient List:

  • 3 Godamba Roti (Sri Lankan flatbreads) or substitute with regular flatbreads
  • 1 cup cooked chicken (or beef, mutton, or vegetables for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or coconut oil)
  • 1 tsp chili powder (optional, adjust to spice preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or coconut oil)
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro (optional)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped

Method:

  1. Chop the roti: Using a sharp knife, cut the roti into thin strips.
  2. Stir-fry the vegetables: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped vegetables and stir-fry until they are tender.
  3. Add the meat: If using meat, add it to the pan and cook until it is lightly browned.
  4. Add the roti: Add the chopped roti to the pan and stir-fry until it is crispy and golden brown.
  5. Add the spices: Add the spices and stir-fry for a few minutes until the flavors are blended.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as curry or sambol.
