Kukul Mas Curry (Sri Lankan Chicken Curry)

Bowl-shaped pancakes, often served with a fried egg or coconut milk in the center.

Ingredient List:

  • Chicken: 1 kg, cut into medium pieces
  • Coconut milk: 1 cup (thick) + 1 cup (thin)
  • Oil: 2-3 tbsp (preferably coconut oil)
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Green chilies: 2-3, slit
  • Curry leaves: 10-12
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece
  • Cardamom pods: 2-3, slightly crushed
  • Cloves: 3-4
  • Fennel seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Mustard seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
  • Turmeric powder: 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder: 1 tbsp
  • Cumin powder: 1 tsp
  • Chili powder: 1-2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Sri Lankan roasted curry powder: 2 tbsp (or use regular curry powder if unavailable)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Lime juice: 1-2 tsp
  • Water: as needed

Method:

  1. Combine the chicken pieces with turmeric powder, a pinch of salt, and 1 tablespoon of roasted curry powder. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and fennel seeds. Fry until aromatic. Add curry leaves, sliced onion, ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Saut√© until the onions turn golden.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until soft and mushy.
  4. Stir in the remaining turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, chili powder, and roasted curry powder. Fry for 1-2 minutes to release the aroma of the spices.
  5. Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Stir well to coat the chicken pieces with the spice mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken begins to brown slightly.
  6. Pour in the thin coconut milk and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add the thick coconut milk and adjust the consistency with water if needed. Simmer for another 5-10 minutes until the chicken is tender and the curry has thickened.
  8. Add lime juice and adjust salt to taste. Garnish with extra curry leaves or chopped cilantro if desired.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice, string hoppers, or roti.
