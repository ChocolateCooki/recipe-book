Ceylon Tea

A rich, aromatic black tea often served with milk and sugar, known for its strong, robust flavor.

Ingredient List:

  • Ceylon tea leaves: 1-2 tsp (adjust depending on how strong you like your tea)
  • Water: 1 cup (approximately 250 ml)
  • Sugar: to taste (optional)
  • Milk: (optional, for milk tea)

Method:

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh water in a kettle or saucepan until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Once the water is boiling, add 1-2 teaspoons of loose Ceylon tea leaves into the boiling water. You can also use a tea strainer or tea infuser to hold the leaves.
  3. Let the tea leaves steep in the boiling water for 3-5 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer your tea. For a lighter tea, steep for less time, and for a stronger flavor, steep for longer.
  4. After the desired steeping time, strain the tea to remove the leaves, pouring the tea into your cup.
  5. If you like your tea sweet, add sugar to taste and stir well. If you prefer milk tea, add a splash of milk (fresh or evaporated milk) and stir to combine. Adjust the amount of milk based on your preference.
  6. Serve the hot Ceylon tea immediately and enjoy its rich, robust flavor!
