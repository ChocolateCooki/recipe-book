Fish Ambul Thiyal

A sour and spicy fish curry made with tamarind, spices, and a mix of Sri Lankan herbs.

Ingredient List:

  • Fish: 500 g (firm varieties like tuna, swordfish, or kingfish), cut into large pieces
  • Goraka (Garcinia cambogia): 3-4 pieces (or tamarind as a substitute)
  • Water: 1/4 cup (to soak goraka)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Pepper: 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder: 1 tsp
  • Curry powder: 1 tbsp (Sri Lankan roasted curry powder preferred)
  • Chili powder: 1-2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, finely grated
  • Curry leaves: 8-10
  • Mustard seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1/4 tsp
  • Green chilies: 2-3, slit
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece
  • Oil: 2-3 tbsp (preferably coconut oil)
  • Water: as needed (about 1/2 cup)

Method:

  1. Soak the goraka pieces in 1/4 cup of warm water for about 10 minutes. Once softened, grind the goraka into a smooth paste. If using tamarind, soak and extract a thick pulp
  2. In a bowl, mix the fish with turmeric powder, chili powder, curry powder, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or clay pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds, and let them splutter. Add garlic, ginger, curry leaves, green chilies, and the cinnamon stick. Saut√© until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the goraka paste (or tamarind pulp) and cook for 1-2 minutes. Add a little water to prevent burning.
  5. Gently place the marinated fish pieces into the pan. Spoon the curry base over the fish to coat it evenly.
  6. Add water to the pan (enough to just cover the fish), and bring it to a gentle simmer. Cover the pan and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse and the fish to cook through.
  7. Remove the lid and let the curry simmer uncovered for another 5-10 minutes until the sauce thickens and clings to the fish.
  8. Serve the Fish Ambul Thiyal with steamed rice and side dishes like dhal curry, pol sambol, or mallung.
