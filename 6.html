Pittu

Steamed mixture of rice flour and grated coconut, served with karamelized coconut sambol or curry.

Ingredient List:

  • Rice flour: 2 cups (roasted for best results)
  • Grated coconut: 1 cup (fresh or desiccated)
  • Grated coconut: 1 cup (fresh or desiccated)
  • Water: 1/2 to 3/4 cup (as needed)
  • Pittu mould (bamboo or stainless steel): optional, for traditional shaping
  • Banana leaf or cloth: optional, for lining the steamer

Method:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rice flour and salt. Gradually sprinkle water into the flour while mixing with your fingers. The texture should resemble damp breadcrumbs or wet sand. It should hold its shape when squeezed but crumble when pressed.
  2. Mix the grated coconut into the moistened rice flour evenly. Ensure the coconut is distributed throughout the mixture.
  3. If using a traditional pittu mould, lightly grease it. For a regular steamer, line it with a banana leaf or a clean cloth to prevent sticking.
  4. Fill the mould or steamer with alternating layers of the rice flour mixture and grated coconut, starting and ending with coconut. Do not press the mixture too tightly; it should remain airy.
  5. Steam over medium heat for about 10-15 minutes or until the pittu is cooked through. The aroma of steamed coconut and rice will indicate it's ready.
  6. Carefully remove the pittu from the mould or steamer. Serve warm with coconut milk, dhal curry, chicken curry, or spicy sambol.
