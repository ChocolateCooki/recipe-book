Wade(vada)

Fried doughnuts made from lentils and spices, often served with coconut chutney or sambol.

Ingredient List:

  • Wash the urad dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 3-4 hours or overnight.
  • Drain the soaked lentils and grind them to a smooth, thick paste using minimal water. The batter should be fluffy and not too watery.
  • Transfer the batter to a mixing bowl. Add green chilies, ginger, chopped onions (if using), curry leaves, cumin seeds, black pepper, and salt. Mix well. If the batter feels too soft or runny, mix in 1-2 tablespoons of rice flour to adjust consistency.
  • Wet your hands slightly with water. Take a small portion of the batter, roll it into a ball, and flatten it slightly. Poke a hole in the center to form a doughnut shape (optional).
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Ensure the oil is hot but not smoking.
  • Carefully drop the shaped batter into the hot oil. Fry a few at a time, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and crisp on both sides.
  • Remove the fried vadas with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  • Serve the wade hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or a spicy chili sauce.

Method:

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 6 hours, or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and grind it into a fine batter using a blender or grinder. Add water or coconut milk gradually while grinding to achieve a smooth, slightly thick batter.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine yeast, sugar, and warm water. Let it sit for 10 minutes until the yeast is foamy. Add the yeast mixture to the rice batter along with salt. Mix well and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  4. Heat a special hopper pan (appachatti) over medium heat. If you don't have a hopper pan, you can use a small non-stick skillet.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan. Swirl the pan to coat the sides with a thin layer of batter.
  6. Cover the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center is cooked through.
  7. Carefully remove the hopper from the pan using a spatula. Serve hot with your favorite accompaniments like dhal curry, sambol, or a fried egg.
  8. Experiment with different toppings and fillings to create your own unique hopper variations.
