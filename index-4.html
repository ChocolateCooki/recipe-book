Kiribath (Milk Rice)

A ceremonial dish made with rice cooked in coconut milk, often served with sambol and a sweet coconut mixture

Ingredient List:

  • 2 cups of short-grain rice (Sri Lankan rice such as Samba or Nadu works best)
  • 3 cups of coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 1 Â½ cups of water
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1 tbsp of butter or ghee (optional, for extra richness)
  • 1 tbsp of sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet flavor)

Method:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch
  2. In a large pot, combine the washed rice, water, and coconut milk. Stir to ensure the rice is evenly distributed. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat. Then, lower the heat and let it simmer gently, stirring occasionally.
  3. Cook for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice has absorbed most of the liquid and is tender. Stir occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom
  4. Once the rice is cooked, add the salt, butter (or ghee), and sugar (if using). Mix gently to combine and ensure the rice is creamy and well-seasoned.
  5. Transfer the cooked rice into a flat dish or a mold to shape it into a neat rectangular or round block. Press the rice down gently to compact it. Let it sit for a few minutes to firm up.
  6. Cut the Kiri Bath into squares or serve as a whole block. It is often served with Lunu Miris (spicy onion sambol), Pol Sambol (coconut sambol), or Sri Lankan curry on the side
  7. Tips:For a richer flavor, use fresh coconut milk and adjust the salt and sugar to your preference. Kiri Bath is often prepared for celebrations and special occasions, especially on New Year's Day in Sri Lanka.
