Coconut Sambol(Pol Sambol)

A spicy coconut relish made with grated coconut, chili, onions, and lime, often served as a side with rice or hoppers

Ingredient List:

  • Fresh grated coconut: 1 cup (or desiccated coconut rehydrated with a little warm water)
  • Red chili powder: 1-2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Dried red chilies: 1-2 (optional, for extra heat, ground)
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped or ground
  • Tomato: 1 small, finely chopped (optional)
  • Lime juice: 2-3 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Maldivian fish flakes: 1-2 tbsp (optional, for added flavor)
  • Curry leaves: 4-5, finely chopped (optional, for garnish)

Method:

  1. Grate the fresh coconut if not already done. If using desiccated coconut, rehydrate it by soaking it in a little warm water for 5-10 minutes.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine grated coconut, red chili powder, and salt.If using dried red chilies, grind them finely and mix into the coconut for an extra spicy flavor.
  3. Mix in the finely chopped onion and tomato. This adds a juicy texture to the sambol.
  4. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the mixture and mix well. Adjust the lime juice based on your preference for tanginess.
  5. If desired, add Maldivian fish flakes for a savory umami flavor. Sprinkle curry leaves for a fresh aroma and garnish.
  6. Taste the sambol and adjust the salt, chili, or lime juice as needed.
