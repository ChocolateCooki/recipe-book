String Hoppers (Idiyappam)

Steamed rice noodles, usually served with dhal curry, coconut sambol, or lunu miris (spicy onion sambol).

Ingredient List:

  • Rice flour: 2 cups (preferably roasted rice flour or idiyappam flour)
  • Hot water: 1 1/2 cups (as needed for kneading)
  • Salt: 1/2 tsp (or to taste)
  • Oil: 1 tsp (optional, for smoother dough)

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the rice flour and salt. Gradually add hot water while mixing with a spoon or spatula. Be careful, as the water is hot. Once the mixture is cool enough to handle, knead it into a soft, smooth, non-sticky dough. Add a little oil if needed to make it pliable.
  2. Grease the inside of the string hopper (idiyappam) press with a little oil to prevent sticking. Fill the press with a portion of the dough.
  3. Grease idiyappam steaming trays or plates lightly with oil. Press the dough through the idiyappam press in a circular motion onto the trays, forming thin noodle-like discs.
  4. Place the trays in a steamer. Steam for about 8-10 minutes on medium heat, or until the string hoppers are cooked and soft. Remove from the steamer and allow them to cool slightly before removing.
  5. Serve warm with coconut sambol, dhal curry, pol (coconut) curry, or any spicy gravy of your choice.
