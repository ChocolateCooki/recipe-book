Lamprais

A Dutch-influenced dish that includes rice, meat curry, sambol, and a boiled egg, all wrapped in a banana leaf and baked

Ingredient List:

    For the Rice:

  • Samba rice (or short-grain rice): 2 cups
  • Coconut milk: 2 cups
  • Chicken or beef stock: 2 cups
  • Salt: to taste
  • Pandan leaf: 1 piece
  • Curry leaves: 6-8

    • For the curry (Meat or chicken):

  • Chicken, beef, or mutton: 500 g, cut into small pieces
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 4-5 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Curry leaves: 6-8
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Chili powder: 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder: 1 tbsp
  • Roasted curry powder: 1 tbsp
  • Cardamom pods: 2-3
  • Cloves: 2-3
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece
  • Tomato: 1, chopped
  • Thick coconut milk: 1 cup
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: 2-3 tbsp

    • For the Sambols and Side Dishes:

  • Fried Ash Plantains: 2, sliced and fried
  • Brinjal Moju (Eggplant Pickle): 250 g, cut into strips and fried
  • Seeni Sambol: 2 large onions, caramelized with spices
  • Prawn Blachan (shrimp paste): 100 g

    • For Wrapping:

  • Banana leaves: cleaned and cut into large rectangles

Method:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly. Combine the rice with coconut milk, stock, salt, pandan leaf, and curry leaves in a pot. Cook until the rice is soft and fluffy. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and curry leaves. Sauté until aromatic. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until golden. Stir in turmeric, chili, coriander, and roasted curry powder. Add the meat and coat well with the spices. Add chopped tomatoes, salt, and coconut milk. Simmer until the meat is tender and the gravy is thick.
  3. Deep-fry the brinjal strips until golden. Set aside. Sauté onions with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and vinegar. Mix in fried brinjal and cook briefly.
  4. Caramelize onions with sugar, tamarind, chili powder, and curry leaves. Cook until sticky and rich.
  5. Grind prawns into a paste. Sauté with garlic, onions, chili, and shrimp paste until fragrant and dry.
  6. Lightly wilt the banana leaves over an open flame to make them pliable. Place a portion of rice in the center of each leaf. Add a spoonful of each curry, sambol, and side dish on top or alongside the rice. Fold the banana leaf into a neat parcel and secure with twine if needed.
  7. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Place the wrapped parcels on a baking tray and bake for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. Serve warm, allowing each person to open their fragrant banana-leaf parcel
