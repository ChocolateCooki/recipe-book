Wood Apple Juice

A tangy and slightly sweet drink made from the pulp of the wood apple fruit, often enjoyed for its unique flavor and health benefits.

Ingredient List:

  • Wood apple (Kurunda): 1 large, ripe
  • Water: 2 cups (adjust based on desired consistency)
  • Sugar: 2-3 tbsp (optional, to taste)
  • Lime juice: 1-2 tsp (optional, for added tanginess)
  • Ice cubes: (optional, for a chilled drink)

Method:

  1. Cut the wood apple in half. Scoop out the soft pulp from the shell using a spoon. Place the pulp in a bowl or blender.
  2. Using a spoon or masher, mash the pulp to release the juice and break it into smaller pieces. You can use a strainer or cheesecloth to separate the juice from the fibers if you prefer a smoother drink.
  3. Add the water to the mashed pulp. Blend the mixture using a hand blender or regular blender until well combined. If you like a smoother texture, strain the juice using a fine mesh sieve to remove any excess pulp.
  4. Add sugar to taste and stir well. You can also add a little lime juice if you prefer a tangy kick.
  5. If desired, add ice cubes to the juice or chill it in the refrigerator before serving.
  6. Pour the wood apple juice into glasses and serve chilled.
